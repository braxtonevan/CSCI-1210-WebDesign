Mossy Frogs

Behavior

The Mossy Frog is a species found in northern Vietnam. While these creatues may look harmless, they are actually one of the most venmous species of frogs in the world. Active at night, these semi-aquatic frogs spend most of the day hiding in the water under rocks or attached to niches in rock outcroppings where they appear to be part of the moss growing on the rock. Mossy frogs hunt large insects such as crickets and cockroaches They also attach to plants floating on the surface of the stream.

Habitat

The Mossy Frog is a species found in northern Vietnam. Vietnamese mossy frogs are found in flooded caves and in the banks of rocky mountain streams at elevations of 2,300 to 3,280 feet (700 to 1,000 meters). They are extremely cold tolerant, however, and can handle temperatures down into the 50's. Mossy Frogs inhabit mountainous, evergreen primary rainforests, where there are exposed limestone outcroppings that have been cut through the steep cliffs by mountain streams.
